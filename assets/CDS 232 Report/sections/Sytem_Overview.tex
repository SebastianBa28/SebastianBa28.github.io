\section{\textbf{System Overview}}

\noindent Autonomous aircraft have demonstrated unparalleled versatility and scalability for distributed tasks in areas such as agriculture, surveillance, delivery, surveying and more. In this project, we study a quadcopter modeled as a rigid body with three degrees of freedom, subject to thrust and moment inputs generated by four independently actuated rotors. The following document details a derivation of the vehicle dynamics, describing its translation and orientation.

\noindent One of the research goals of this system is to develop controllers that ensure safety guarantees under disturbance uncertainty. For instance, consider a drone used for aerial package delivery. As the drone travels, the weight of the packages will act as a swinging pendulum, disturbing the natural flight of the aircraft. This disturbance will inherently be influenced by the weight and the geometry of the payload. How can we design a controller robust to this uncertainty?
